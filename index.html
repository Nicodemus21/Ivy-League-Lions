INTERSTING FACTS ABOUT LIONS

The Lions in Africa are the most social of all the big cats and live in prides consisting of about 15 lions.

The male lions are in charge of defending their pride's territorial boundary while the lionesses do most of the hunting though the lion eats first.

Earlier, lions could be found in Africa, Europe and Asia, however, today they are only found in Africa with the Indian Sasan-Gir Park being the only exception.

Although lions are mostly referred to as the king of the jungle they actually only habit plains and grasslands.

The lion's age can be gauged by the darkness of his mane. The darker the mane, the older the lion.

The lion's roar can be heard from as far as 5 miles away

A lion can sleep for up to 20 hours a day, their heels also do not touch the ground when they walk and they can run short distances at a speed of 50 mph.

ENDANGERED LIONS' DOCUMENTARY

We are facing extinction, ACT NOW!!

According to IUCN Red List African lions are an endangered species that is now vulnerable to extinction. Their survival is threatened by the rapid loss and fragmentation of their habitat. Lions are also killed by humans during bravery rituals, for medicinal powers, by ranchers protecting their livestock and as hunting trophies.

Moreover, the lions are susceptible to tick-borne diseases such as babesia and distemper. Babesia infection occurs during droughts when malnourished prey are more susceptible to disease. On the other hand, distemper is passed to lions by hyenas and neighboring village dogs. Lions get infected once they kill the sick prey. The combination of babesia and distemper is responsible for mass fatalities in lion populations.

Therefore, lets donate more to conservation agencies in Africa to ensure they are more protected and treated of Babesia and Distemper since all human generations need to see a living lion.
